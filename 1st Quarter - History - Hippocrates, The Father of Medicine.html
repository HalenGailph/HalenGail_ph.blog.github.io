History and Science - " Hippocrates, The Father Of Medicine "

Hippocrates is one of the famous Greek philosophers. He was also known as the father of medicine even though he didn't invent medicine. It was because he greatly changed the views of how people think about medicine. Hippocrates approached medicine more scientifically than religiously, which was right. For example, the Greeks believed that if somebody was sick, it was because the anger of the (Greek) gods was upon the person. Well, Hippocrates would examine the person and try to find the cause of his/her illness. Then he would prescribe herbs, fresh air, diet changes, and/or exercise. Sounds like a doctor, doesn't it?

._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._._.

But Hippocrates wasn't always right. He believe that in order to be healthy, you had to have equal amounts of 4 humors. These humors were also known as the 4 liquids. Some people believed that these humors could determine your pesonality. The humors were*:

" Phlegmatic ( phlegm ) : Easy going and easy to get along with, usually happy. "

" Sanguine ( blood ) : Excited, full of energy, usually happy. "

" Choleric ( yellow bile ) : Controlling, leader, likes to be in charge. "

" Melancholy ( black bile ) : Thoughtful, artistic, emotional. "

Another thing we can get from Hippocrates is the " Hippocratic Oath ." This oath was written some time after his death in 377 B. C. This oath is also a standard for doctors to live by.** And this is where Ben Carson comes in. As written in the book, " THE MYSTERY OF HISTORY: Volume 1: Creation to the Resurrection ,"** the American Medical Association uses this oath as a pledge for doctors graduating from medical school. Ben Carson is a doctor, so he must have said the oath.
I'm going to show you is a modern version of the Oath.

The Oath of Hippocrates**

I do solemnly swear by that which I hold most sacred:
That I will be loyal to the Profession of Medicine and just and generous to its Members:
That I will lead my life and practice my Art in uprightness and honor:
That into whatsoever house I will enter, it shall be for the good of the sick and the well to the utmost of my power, and that I will hold myself aloof from wrong and from corruption, from the tempting of others to vice:
I will exercise my Art solely for the cure of my patients and will give no drug, perform no operation for criminal purpose and far less suggest a thing.
That whatsoever I shall see or hear in the lives of men which is not fitting to be spoken, I will keep inviolably secret. These things I do swear.
These things I do promise and in the proportion as I am faithful to this oath, may hapiness and good repute be ever mine, the opposite if I shall be foresworn.
